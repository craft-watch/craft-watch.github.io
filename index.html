Northern Monk
14.06 Fingathing

Can Of Whup Ass // Australian DDH IPA

NEW !!!

6.7% ABV // 1.069 OG // 7 EBC // 20 IBU // 440ML

INGREDIENTS: Malted BARLEY (GLUTEN), OATS (GLUTEN), Maltodextrin, Hops, Yeast

While the hustle of the North American hop harvest is just getting going across the Atlantic, in the UK we’re able to turn our hop buds to the southern hemisphere for a few months as the fresh 2020 harvest selections from New Zealand and Australia arrive in our cold stores.

Often, wrongly, overshadowed by the more famous US varieties and their larger acreage, there’s plenty of unique and intense flavours to be found in the Southern Hemisphere. So, for this Northern Sounds collaboration with Fingathing, we’ve put together this ‘Can of Whup Ass’, a DDH IPA to showcase the best of Australian hops.

We started with a base of extra pale barley malt backed up with large volumes of naked and flaked oats and some maltodextrin, all there to flesh out the body and bring that desirable silky mouthfeel. In the whirlpool, there’s a small US contribution in the shape of some Citra Incognito, which is partnered with a big addition of Enigma T90, the dankest of the Aussie varieties but also contributor of some prominent passion fruit notes. At the back end of fermentation came a 28g/l dry hop led by Galaxy and Vic Secret, which enhances the passion fruit aroma and brings juicy tangerine, lychee, and ripe tropical fruit.

© Northern Monk

Orbit
Sticke Alt

NEW !!!

Inspired by the legend of the Sticke Fest and Sticke Alt. Twice annually, a special strong version of the Dusseldorf Altbier is brewed by three breweries in the Dusseldorf Altstadt (old town). The beer is put on the bar (literally, wooden barrels are hoisted up on to the bar and tapped) twice a year and enjoyed only by locals lucky enough to be in the know regarding the availability of this special release.

Our homage is coppery red with notes of toffee, raisin, bread, spice and herbs. A full body, aggressive bitterness and long, clean finish thanks to the lagering time. A particular nod to Zum Uerige Sticke Altbier, traditionally drunk on the third Tuesday in January and October, as well as ‘Neu’ Orbit’s original strength Altbier from our back catalogue.
