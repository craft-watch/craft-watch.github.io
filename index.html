Pressure Drop
Parachute 5.5% New England Pale

NEW !!!

ABV: 5.5%

Style: New England Pale Ale

Hops: Ekuanot, Citra, HBC431

juciy • soft • bright

Sometimes you have to go with the flow, and when the flow is delicious hoppy, hazy beer then its not such a bad thing. Our mission for Parachute was to create a soft stage for the hops to dance on. We focused on the idea of softness and worked on the water profile to achieve the mouthfeel we were after. Low bitterness was key to the plan, as was a grain bill with the inclusion of oats and wheat to build the texture of the beer.

The hop selection is Ekuanot, HBC431 and Citra, double dry-hopped at a rate of 16g/L. The beer is gold coloured and hazy, with a rich aroma of hops, low bitterness and a soft palate that allows the hops to fully express their fruity goodness.

First Verse, Twice

DDH IPA

NEW !!!

First Verse, Twice

Range: Augment

Style: DDH India Pale Ale

ABV: 6.4%

We confess to being big music heads here at Polly’s HQ, with a huge spectrum of styles appreciated brewery-wide. Whether it’s a “Champagne Supernova”, a “Killing in the Name”, or an “Orange Crush”, there’s just something that’s always stuck in our minds about how uniquely memorable a repeated first verse is. We wanted to apply this concept to a beer, and First Verse, Twice was born. Featuring a first verse of El Dorado in the boil, we added a healthy chorus of Mosaic and Simcoe during fermentation, before coming back for another go around on El Dorado once again in the dry-hop. The result is a juicebomb of a beer, positively dripping with notes of tangerine, jelly tots (yes, really) and red grapefruit for balance. An all out explosion of flavour; Team Polly’s doing what we do best

Surface Edition

IPA

NEW !!!

Surface Edition

Range: Augment

Style: India Pale Ale

ABV: 7.0%

We’ve always had a thing here at the brewery for those big, brash IPAs north of 7% brewed by those old school brewers that we grew up drinking. Those base flavours from the malts, accentuated by the flavour profiles brought to the table by the hops, they all come together as the strength heads into the upper echelons of IPA territory. As a brewery that firmly embraces the past in our brewing style, we’ve employed our pyramid dry-hopping technique here with a heavy dose of the present in Azacca, before a nod to the past with a smaller but still healthy addition of Chinook, finishing with an eye on the future with a further small addition of Sabro. Expect tropical juice to meld with pina colada sweetness before finishing out with piney bitterness in this all out smasher of an IPA.
