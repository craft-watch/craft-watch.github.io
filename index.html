Anspach & Hobday
The English Pale Series

NEW !!!

The English Pale series is designed to showcase new English hops. The folks at Yakima Chief Hops have supplied us with Ernest and Endeavour hops before they are released for general sale.

They are looking for your feedback too! If you grab these brand new English beers you'll be sent a postcard with a stamp attached. Simply put down your feedback, your name, and you can be in with a chance to win a free case!

Each beer focuses on a different hop and is brewed using the same simple malt bill and clean yeast strain. The beer is custom-designed to allow the unique character of each hop to shine through. These are 440ml cans. The two beers are split at an equal ratio across all size options.

Shipped from Monday 1st August.

The English Pale: Ernest (4.0%): Originally rejected due to its 'strong, coarse, American aroma', Ernest was bred by Ernest Salmon in 1923. Its "New World" flavour profile of apricot, citrus and spice was not desirable at the time, and it was deemed a poor substitute for the control hop of the trials, Fuggles. In the context of today's brewing, these bold, fruity flavours are more at home, and Ernest could yet prove itself as a valuable addition to the contemporary brewing world.

The English Pale: Endeavour (4.0%): A cross between Cascade and a Hedgerow hop, and a granddaughter of Target, Endeavour is a gentler, yet complex alternative to its forebears. Still uniquely English in character, Endeavour also imparts notable citrus and red-fruit flavours. Trials so far have also noted a well rounded bitterness and subtle hints of spice. Whilst perhaps not as upfront as many New World hops, Endeavour has plenty to offer the brewer seeking complexity, balance and finesse.

Range: Augment

Style: India Pale Ale

ABV: 6.8%

It’s back once again! We harp on all the time about how much we love the old school here at Polly’s whilst simultaneously embracing the new school. It comes as no surprise then that we noticed we had an absolute load of old school C hops in our cold store and a gap in our brew plans needing to be filled for release at the end of July.

A fiendish plot came to fruition when we created The Weekend Radio; a five C-hop showcase of everything old school, featuring heavyweights Citra, Columbus, Centennial, Chinook and Cascade, all wrapped up in our new school fermentation techniques. The result is an IPA packed to the nines with strong citrus notes, but maximum drinkability and softness, echoing the OG craft beer titans, but sitting firmly in the present.
